The original COVID-19 vaccines and boosters have done a great job preventing severe illness and hospitalization. But then came Omicron and its subvariants, which were more transmissible than the other strains of SARS-CoV-2. And because the Omicron BA.5 subvariant evaded infection and vaccine-provoked immunity, breakthrough infections and reinfections have become more common as the virus evolves. Advancing public health The AMA leads the charge on public health. Our members are the frontline of patient care, expanding access to care for underserved patients and developing key prevention strategies.
